# 🛡️ Part B – Prevention Checklist: Digital Safety

### 🔒 Stay Safe Online Checklist


* **💳 The "PIN is for Paying" Rule (UPI Safety):** Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If a buyer on a platform like OLX or a random contact sends you a QR code and asks you to enter your PIN to "credit" your account, it is a 100% scam.
 
* **🔎 Verify UPI IDs Before Clicking 'Pay':** Before completing any transaction on GPay, PhonePe, or Paytm, double-check the verified name of the recipient. Scammers often use names that look like official college offices or popular brands to trick students.
 
* **🔐 Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Activate 2FA on your Gmail, LinkedIn, and GitHub accounts. Use an authenticator app rather than SMS to prevent "SIM swapping" attacks which are increasingly common.
 
* **🌐 Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks:** Do not log into your bank account or student portal using free campus or cafe Wi-Fi. If you must use it, ensure you are using a trusted VPN to encrypt your data.
 
* **📱 Audit App Permissions:** Regularly check your smartphone settings to ensure third-party "photo editing" or "utility" apps do not have unnecessary access to your contacts, microphone, or SMS.
 
* **🔒 Practice "HTTPS" Vigilance:** Before entering your credentials on any website—especially for exam registrations or fee payments—ensure the URL starts with https:// and shows the padlock icon.
 
* **🔑 Use a Dedicated Password Manager:** Avoid using the same password for HackerRank and your personal email. Use a manager to create complex, unique passwords for every academic and professional platform.
 
* **📩 Beware of "Job Offer" Phishing:** Be skeptical of unsolicited messages on WhatsApp or Telegram offering "part-time data entry" or "easy money." These are often phishing attempts to steal your identity or banking details.

### 🚨 How to Report Cybercrime in India
If you fall victim to a scam or notice suspicious activity, immediate reporting is your best chance at recovering funds or securing your identity:

* **🌐 Official Portal:** File a complaint at the **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal** (cybercrime.gov.in).
* **📞 Emergency Helpline:** Call **1930** immediately to report financial fraud and initiate the process of freezing fraudulent transactions.
