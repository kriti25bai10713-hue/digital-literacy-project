# 🛡️ Part A – Case Study: Phishing

### 🎣 Phishing Overview
Phishing is one of the most common forms of cybercrime, where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or credit card numbers. It typically happens through fraudulent emails, text messages, or fake websites designed to look legitimate.

---

### ⚙️ How it happens (step‑by‑step):
1. **The Preparation:** The attacker creates a fake email or website that closely resembles a trusted source, such as a bank or e‑commerce site.
2. **The Hook:** The victim receives the message, often containing urgent language like *“Your account will be blocked”* or *“Verify your details immediately.”*
3. **The Trap:** The victim clicks on the malicious link and is redirected to a fake login page.
4. **The Catch:** Once the victim enters their credentials, the attacker captures the information and uses it for financial fraud or identity theft.

---

### 👥 Who is targeted:  
Phishing attacks usually target everyday internet users, especially those less familiar with digital security practices. **Students, professionals, and even large organizations** can fall victim if they are not cautious.

### ⚠️ Consequences:  
The consequences of phishing can be severe. Victims may lose money through unauthorized transactions, suffer identity theft, or have their accounts misused. For organizations, phishing can lead to data breaches, reputational damage, and financial losses.

### 📊 Example: 
> In 2020, **Google reported blocking over 100 million phishing emails daily**, highlighting the scale of this threat. This shows that phishing is not an isolated incident but a widespread cybercrime affecting millions worldwide.
